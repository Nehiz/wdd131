Website Planning Document: Pi and Beads – Abacus, Coding, and AI for Kids

A WDD 131 Project by Nehikhare Efehi

Site Name

Pi and Beads

The name "Pi and Beads" reflects the core focus of the site: combining the mathematical concept of "Pi" (symbolizing math and logic) with "Beads" (representing the abacus, a tactile learning tool). The inclusion of AI and coding in the program adds a modern tech dimension, making it a holistic learning experience for kids. I chose this name because it’s memorable, playful, and ties to the educational themes of abacus math, coding, and AI, appealing to both parents and children.

Potential domain name: piandbeads.com

Site Purpose

The purpose of the Pi and Beads website is to promote an interactive learning program for kids that combines AI, coding, and abacus math. The site will serve as a hub for parents to learn about the program’s benefits, explore the AI, coding, and abacus math tracks, and request more information via a contact form. It will provide detailed program information, interactive features like a quiz and coding demos, and a gallery of kids engaging with the tools, all designed to engage and inform the target audience.

Scenarios

These scenarios represent questions that parents (my target audience) might ask when visiting the site:

  • What are the benefits of the abacus math program for my 7-year-old, and what age group is it best suited for?
  • What kind of coding projects will my child learn, and can I see an example of a project they might create?
  • How does the AI track introduce artificial intelligence to kids, and what simple AI concepts will my child explore?

Color Schema

The site will use a bright, playful color scheme to appeal to kids and parents, while ensuring accessibility through high contrast ratios:

  • Sky Blue (#4A90E2): Used for headers, navigation bar background, buttons, and accents to create a friendly, approachable feel, reflecting the tech aspect of AI and coding.
  • Sunshine Yellow (#F5A623): Used for call-to-action buttons (e.g., "Take the Quiz"), highlights, and hover effects to add energy and draw attention, symbolizing the creativity of learning.
  • White (#FFFFFF): Used for the main background to ensure readability and contrast with text and colored elements.
  • Dark Gray (#333333): Used for body text and footer text to ensure high contrast against the white background.

These colors are applied in this site plan document to demonstrate their usage.

Typography

The site will use two fonts to balance readability and playfulness:

  • Poppins (400, 700 weights): Used for headings (e.g., h1, h2, h3) to create a modern, rounded look that’s kid-friendly. Weight 400 for regular headings and weight 700 for emphasis (e.g., page titles).
  • Open Sans (400 weight): Used for body text and paragraphs to ensure high readability, especially for parents reading program details.

These fonts are applied in this site plan document to demonstrate their usage.
